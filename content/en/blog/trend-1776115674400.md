## 💡 Last Check Q&A Before You Decide

**Is the cost difference really that big if I only use Superchargers?**  
No. If you rely 100% on Superchargers, your savings will drop significantly. Supercharging is convenient but expensive—often approaching the cost of cheap gas. This math only works in your favor if you can charge at home for the bulk of your miles. If you can't charge at home or work, stick to gas for now.

**What about the battery replacement cost everyone talks about?**  
It's a non-issue for a 50,000-mile comparison. Tesla batteries are warrantied for 100,000 to 120,000 miles depending on the model. You are protected. Worrying about a battery swap at 50k miles is like worrying about your gas engine exploding the day after you buy it. It's possible, but the data says it won't happen.
