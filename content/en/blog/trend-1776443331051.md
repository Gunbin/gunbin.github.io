### The Bottom Line (BLUF)
Being physically fit is not a hall pass for longevity. Statistics show that 50% of sudden, life-threatening heart events occur in individuals who had **zero** prior symptoms. For men in their 50s—including elite former athletes like the legendary Angels outfielder who recently passed at just 53—a standard blood pressure check isn't enough. You need specialized imaging that looks at actual arterial buildup, not just lifestyle indicators.

### Why "Looking Good" is a Dangerous Metric
Most of us think that if we aren't winded on the stairs and our cholesterol is "fine," we’re safe. That’s a myth. Professional athletes and high-performers often have high pain tolerances and bodies that compensate for issues until they can't anymore. This is the "Sudden 50s Cliff"—a period where asymptomatic heart disease finally catches up to the plumbing.

### The Bottleneck: Why Your Doctor Might Miss It
The biggest hurdle isn't the science; it's the protocol. Most standard physicals focus on "risk factors" (weight, smoking, BP). If you look healthy, many doctors won't order the deep-dive tests.

**Common Errors to Avoid:**
*   **The "Fit Guy" Bias:** Assuming because you exercise, your arteries are clear.
*   **The Stress Test Trap:** Thinking a treadmill test proves you're safe (it often only catches blockages over 70%).
*   **Insurance Waiting Games:** Waiting for a symptom to appear before asking for a scan. (By then, it's often too late).
